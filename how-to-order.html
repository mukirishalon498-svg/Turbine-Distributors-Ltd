Order Now

Order Now

Discover our step-by-step ordering process, from initial sourcing and quality checks to packing, shipping, and final delivery. This guide explains what you should provide at each stage and what to expect along the way.

Sourcing

Step 12-4 business days

We identify the right raw materials and suppliers based on your product requirements, origin preferences, and budget. You should provide your desired commodity grade, quantity, and destination, and our team will confirm the best available shipment window.

Quality Check

Step 21-3 business days

Our quality team evaluates samples and inspects the goods against agreed specifications. Clients may need to provide test criteria or certification requirements, and we share a detailed inspection report before moving the order into storage or loading.

Storage

Step 31-2 days

Goods are stored in secure facilities with the appropriate conditions for your commodity type. During this stage, we confirm packaging, inventory levels, and readiness for shipment, while you review the final loading schedule and documentation.

Trading / Contract

Step 43-5 business days

We finalize the commercial terms, agree on price, payment method, and shipping conditions, then issue the sales contract. You will review and approve the contract, provide payment instructions, and confirm the chosen Incoterms for the shipment.

Packing

Step 52-3 business days

The goods are packed according to export and transport requirements, including secure sealing, labeling, and palletizing if needed. We send you packing lists, and you can request any special handling instructions prior to dispatch.

Shipping

Step 6Varies by route

We coordinate the transport leg, book carriers, and prepare export documentation for departure. You may need to confirm customs details and payment instructions, while we keep you informed of the vessel schedule and estimated time of arrival.

Delivery

Step 7Final delivery window

Once the goods clear customs, we arrange final delivery to the agreed destination or warehouse. You receive shipping confirmations, arrival notices, and handover documentation so you can smoothly take possession of the cargo.

Frequently Asked Questions

MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)

Our MOQ depends on the commodity and origin, but most orders start from a reasonable bulk lot size. Please contact us with your preferred volume and product type, and we will confirm the minimum quantity.

Payment Terms

We typically accept Letter of Credit (LC) and Telegraphic Transfer (TT) for international orders. Specific terms are agreed during contracting and can be tailored to your payment preference and risk profile.

Incoterms Used

We commonly trade on FOB and CIF terms, depending on whether you prefer to handle the freight or want us to include shipping costs to the destination port. We explain the responsibilities and costs for each option before you confirm the order.

Sample Availability

Samples can usually be arranged upon request, subject to product availability and logistics timing. We advise requesting them early so quality checks and approvals can happen before the full shipment is booked.
